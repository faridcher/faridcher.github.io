Geo is derived from the latin `ge` meaning the Earth, its surface and everything near its surface. One may encounter various Geo- disciplines that may lead to their confusion; these include geoscience, geography, geographical information system (GIS), geographical information science (GIScience), geoinformatics, geospatial (used interchangeably with spatial, however, spatial is a broader term meaning "related to space encompassing position, shape and size of things" and not necessarily geo), geostatistics, geomatics, geocomputataion, geology, geophysics and geodesy (also called geodetics). 

Geodesy is the core disciplines that has its roots in mathematics, physics and computer science; a field that deals with the measurement and representation of earth, its gravitational field and geodynamics phenomena. Geography, meaning describing the earth, is the study of the locational and spatial variation of both natural and human phenomena on earth. Geometry literally means "earth measurement" but it is commonly known as a subfield of mathematics that deals with angles, shapes formed by the relationships of lines, surfaces, and solid objects in space.

Geology has two major subfields namely historical geology and physical geology (geophysics). Historical geology deals with origin of the earth and its development. Physical geology examines the materials composing Earth and seeks to understand the many processes that operate beneath and upon its surface.

Geostatistics applies the theory of stochastic process and inferential statistics to geographic phenomena. Spatial statistics (also called spatial analysis) includes formal techniques that study entities using their topological, geometric, or geographic properties. 

The buzzwords Geoscience, Geocomputataion, GIS, GIScience, Geospatial science, Geomatics and Geoinformatics are the same and are broader terms that encompass all disciplines related to study of the planet earth.
